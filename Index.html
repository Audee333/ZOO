Sloth Bear

Despite their name, sloth bears are not related to sloths. Native to the Indian subcontinent, they use their long, curved claws to dig for termites
and ants — two of their favorite foods in the wild. Jagger, a 3-year-old female, arrived at the Detroit Zoo in October 2024 and now resides behind the grizzly bears in the America Grasslands habitat.

  1. Honey
  2. Sunny

Giraffes

The Detroit Zoo is home to four giraffes – males Mpenzi and Jabari, and females Kivuli and Zara. Giraffes are the tallest land mammals
in the world with long legs and necks, narrow faces, and two fur covered horns, or ossicones, adorning their heads.

  2. Chloe

Alligator

Alligator newts are originally native to evergreen forests, grasslands and swamps in Japan and Taiwan. They are now extinct in
Taiwan. At the Detroit Zoo, these amphibians thrive in a habitat that has plenty of space to burrow.
